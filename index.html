Hexapod Robot

Oct 19th, 2014 3:00 pm

Hexapod Robot

Inspired by the prosperity and variety of the contemporary field of hexapod robot, our group had decided to make one that can move forward and backward. There are many types of hexapod robots and we decided to start with the simplest, therefore a design which is based on four-link mechanism was incorporated in our final design.

We started this project from scratch. After doing much researching, we got to know more about the state of art and in the end we came up with a design filled with our teammates’ ingenuity and creativity. Based on the design, we started the assembly. Our research method is mainly hands-on, which means we find a problem when we are tinkering and come up with a solution. The main ways of manufacturing the parts involves lathe turning, drilling, metal bending, sawing, mechanical cutting, laser cutting, soldering, adhesive and mechanical fastenings including screws and bolts, clamp spring restraining. Due to the fact that we were lack of engineering experiences and knowledge, we ran into many difficulties. Thanks to the teachers in the engineering training center, we solved all the problems successfully despite all the stumbles during the process.

In the end, our team had manufactured a hexapod that can move forward and backward, and turn left or right. The basic structure of the robot consists of two parts: mechanical part and electrical part. The mechanical part involves the legs, the body and the gears; the electrical part includes the motors, the circuit and the battery. Together, they bring the hexapod robot into life.

The four-link mechanism makes it possible for this robot to move with six legs driven by only two motors. Compared to other kinds of hexapod robots, this robot is definitely primitive, yet there is beauty lies in its simplicity.

Keywords: hexapod robot, four-link mechanism, machining process technology

The Research of Vision System Based Autonomous Quadrotor Cruise

Introduction

Quadcopters have been utilized in many fields and one of them is power grid cruise. The objective of this project is to develop a mechanical arm that can automatically hold on to the power cable with visual feedback from the camera mounted on the top of the quad. Solidworks was used in designing and simulating motion of the mechanical arms and Python script was used together with OpenCV extension in annalyzing visual signals and motion control.

Plannar Motor

Oct 19th, 2014 12:46 pm

Large-stroke Piezo-actuated Plannar Motor for Nanopositioning Application

Plannar Motor Configuration

Abstract

This paper presents a large-stroke piezo-actuated planar motor for nano-positioning applications. The planar motor uses an air bearing with vacuum preload to reduce friction. Four piezo legs located at each corner of the mover can perform three operation modes to drive the planar motor, the skiing mode, the stepping mode, and the tilting mode. The skiing mode is a high-speed actuation mode. The stepping mode provides high-resolution trajectory tracking capability. The tilting mode allows for fine adjustment of the stage in its final position with the highest resolution. These three operation modes have different positioning advantages and can be combined to achieve both a high positioning speed and a high positioning resolution. This planar motor has large stroke that is only limited by the base size and large rotation range up to 360°. Two measurement systems based on flat panel display are developed to evaluate the motion performance of the planar motor including motion resolution and trajectory tracking accuracy.

Keywords

planar motor, stage, positioning system, piezo, flexure hinge, planar encoder, semiconductor production
